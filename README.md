## Philosophy

I build at the intersection of **structured systems** and **human meaning-making**.

The work spans from tarot symbolism to real estate transactions to e-commerce workflows, but the underlying question is always the same: how do you make implicit knowledge explicit and machine-actionable without collapsing the depth?

Structured schemas are infrastructure for this. AI systems trained on well-formalized symbolic systems (like tarot) don't "believe in tarot" — they learn how humans encode and reason about ambiguity using formal structures. That generalizes.

Whether it's:
- Mapping Bangkok property listings to semantic schemas with environmental risk encoded
- Structuring tarot spreads so AI can understand positional semantics, not just keyword matching  
- Building review aggregation systems that route sentiment intelligently

...the work is about making systems legible to machines while preserving what matters to humans.
